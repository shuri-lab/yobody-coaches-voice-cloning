01
01Good to know

We capture how you sound, not what you say.

Your voice is built from the sound of you, your tone, your warmth, the rhythm of how you speak. It is never built from your actual words, and nothing you talk about is reused or repeated. So the topic honestly doesn't matter. Talk about your week, tell a story, say whatever feels natural. There are no wrong answers.

02
02Before you record

Set up a quiet space.

A quiet room is all it takes. Three quick things to set up before you start.

Find your quietest room
Soft spaces with carpet, curtains, or a sofa are best. Avoid big, empty rooms that echo.
Record on two phones, both on airplane mode
Use one to start and pause, and leave the other running the whole time as a backup, just in case.
Hold them a forearm away
Keep the phones about a forearm from your mouth, not too close and not too far.
02
02While you record

Talk naturally, about anything.

Speak the way you would to a client or a friend, about whatever you like. Keep going until you reach about 90 minutes.

Pick something and just talk
A minute or two on anything, as if the person is right there with you. Don't prepare, and don't aim for perfect.
Let your energy move
Fired up one moment, gentle the next, the way it really does. That range is exactly what we want.
Take breaks whenever you like
Short 10 to 15 minute sessions add up fast, so there's no need to do it all at once.

There's no need to worry about perfect wording, sounding polished, or what you talk about. None of that matters here.
